Kat Ellinger and Samm Deighan open their commentary admitting their love for the film--Deighan even named her blog after it--but their commentary does more than gush. They contextualize the film within the nunsploitation genre--pointing out how it lacks many of the common tropes--and provide fascinating backgrounds on Enrique Rocha and Cecilia Pezet. Unfortunately, details about the actual production remain scarce--a gap they acknowledge. That said, an included interview with Solares' son Adolfo, who co-wrote the picture with his father, offers some insight into the film's inception and scripting, including how the ending was intended as an "open door" to allow the censors to pass the film.
